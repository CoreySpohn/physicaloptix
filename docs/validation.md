# Verification and validation

physicaloptix is checked against independent ground truth, not only against
itself. This page is the evidence: what is asserted, against which truth
source, at what tolerance, and where each check runs. Every number quoted here
is the assertion of a named test in `tests/` or `tests/validation/`, so the
page can be audited by running the suite.

## How the evidence is organized

| Tier | Truth source | Where it runs |
|------|-------------|---------------|
| A -- code verification | Closed-form physics, mathematical identities, property-based invariants, gradient checks, convergence-order pins | Everywhere: CI, every fresh clone, no data needed |
| B -- cross-code benchmark | Reference products generated by the HWO Coronagraph Design Survey pipeline (cds_pipeline, HCIPy) | Locally, when the reference cache is present; these tests **skip silently without it**, so CI does not exercise them |
| C -- physical validation | Laboratory or testbed data | Not yet; see the honesty section below |

The distinction matters: a `pip install physicaloptix && pytest` user gets
Tier A only. Tier A is therefore designed to carry the deep-null trust story
on its own, from analytic theorems, while Tier B ties the library to the
official survey tool at bit-serious precision.

## Tier A: analytic anchors

### The Fourier core

The continuous-FT matrix Fourier transform pair is the foundation: its
normalization is what lets multi-scale vortex levels add coherently and gives
absolute photometry. `test_cmft.py` and `test_airy_absolute.py` pin:

- **Adjoint identity** $\langle F f, g\rangle_u = \langle f, B g\rangle_x$
  under the grids' integration weights, property-based over random grid shapes
  and samplings (hypothesis), to a relative $10^{-12}$.
- **Parseval and round trip** on complete conjugate grids at $10^{-12}$.
- **The Airy pattern, absolutely.** A gray-edge circular pupil transforms to
  $(\pi/4)\,2J_1(\pi r)/(\pi r)$: the peak matches to a relative $10^{-6}$
  (the absolute area normalization), the full **complex** field profile to a
  relative $3\times10^{-4}$ over $\pm6\,\lambda/D$ (catching phase-convention
  errors intensity comparisons cannot), and 83.8 percent of the energy falls
  inside the first null. The profile error **converges at second order** in
  pupil resolution, and the test asserts the order, not only the value.
- **Exact Fourier identities.** An anisotropic pupil tilt shifts the focal
  field by exactly the tilt (asserted at $10^{-12}$; a transposed axis cannot
  pass), a sinusoidal phase grating leaves exactly $J_0(a)$ of the on-axis
  amplitude including near-extinction at the Bessel zero $a = 2.4048$, and a
  real even pupil transforms to a real field at roundoff.
- **The sampling gate formula** (`mft_sampling_parameter`) reproduces the
  Nyquist-critical value on FFT-conjugate grids, scales exactly with focal
  extent and with the chromatic blue-end wavelength ratio, and is symmetric in
  its two directions.

### The vortex coronagraph, from a theorem

An even-charge vortex on an unobstructed circular pupil diffracts all on-axis
light outside the geometric pupil (Mawet et al. 2005; Foo et al. 2005), so
behind an undersized Lyot stop the focal plane is analytically dark -- and odd
charges do not null. `test_vortex_analytic.py` runs this data-free anchor at
design-relevant depth:

- Charges 2, 4, and 6 behind a 0.80 Lyot stop reach a 3-10 $\lambda/D$
  annulus mean contrast below $5\times10^{-12}$ / $10^{-11}$ (measured
  $1.2\text{-}3.3\times10^{-12}$), peak contrast below $2\times10^{-10}$,
  with residual Lyot-plane power around $2\times10^{-6}$ of the incident.
- The **charge-1 negative control** leaks at $5\times10^{-5}$ -- a
  seven-order-of-magnitude discrimination a ladder or sign regression could
  not survive.
- The charge-2 exterior Lyot field matches the closed form
  $-(R/r)^2 e^{2i\theta}$ in amplitude, phase structure, and global sign.
- The floor is shown to be **aperture-edge representation, not the vortex
  machinery**: it improves by two orders when the pupil edge is
  area-supersampled and is flat in the ladder depth.

The deep-null machinery is therefore verified at the $10^{-11}$-contrast
regime in any environment, three orders below the design-survey requirement,
before any reference data enters.

### Near field (Fresnel)

`test_fresnel.py` and `test_fresnel_validation.py` (which needs no external
data and always runs) anchor the angular-spectrum propagator to closed forms:

- The **analytic Gaussian beam** in amplitude and phase to a relative
  $10^{-4}$, the Rayleigh broadening law, and energy conservation to
  $10^{-9}$ across distances.
- The **Talbot phase-to-amplitude conversion law**
  $2a\sin(\pi\alpha\nu_0^2)$ across a frequency-by-distance sweep -- the
  physics that lets an out-of-pupil deformable mirror control amplitude --
  including the regime guard showing it inert at primary-diameter scale and
  order-unity at relay scale.
- **Plane-wave eigenfunctions**: a frequency-lattice carrier emerges
  multiplied by exactly the transfer-function phase (asserted at $10^{-12}$,
  even and odd grid sizes, both the paraxial and exact methods, both
  directions), pinning the frequency bookkeeping at machine precision.
- An independent direct chirp-convolution reference, the
  paraxial-versus-exact gap scaling as $\beta^2$, and the guard-band padding
  mechanism proven equal to a native larger grid.

### Mode bases and wavefront sensing

- **Noll identity** (`test_modes.py`): Zernike mode $j$ is checked against
  the explicit Noll-indexed polynomial (tilts, defocus, both astigmatisms,
  spherical) by normalized correlation above $1-10^{-9}$, with a
  swapped-parity control proving a sine/cosine swap would fail. Unit-RMS
  normalization contracts hold at $10^{-12}$.
- **Zernike wavefront sensor** (`test_zernike_wfs.py`): the sensor response
  matches the full first-order interferometric model
  $\Delta I = 2P\,(b\,\varphi - K\!\ast\!(P\varphi))$, where $b$ is the
  dot-filtered reference wave, to 6 percent at 0.05 rad RMS with the residual
  scaling as $\varphi^2$. The test also pins that the naive gain map
  $2Pb\varphi$ is measurably incomplete for low-order modes (the
  convolutional term is first-order), which is why calibration uses an
  interaction matrix.

### The differentiability contract

Being differentiable is the library's reason to exist, so gradients are
verified, not assumed (`test_gradients.py`):

- `jax.test_util.check_grads` compares forward- **and reverse-mode**
  autodiff against second-order finite differences for dark-zone intensity
  losses through the Fraunhofer chain, the **multi-scale vortex plus Lyot
  chain**, the two-deformable-mirror Fresnel relay, and the
  fractional-amplitude mode map.
- The `linearize` sensitivity column for a Fourier mode equals the
  shift-theorem closed form $i(2\pi/\lambda)(a/2)[F_0(u-f)+F_0(u+f)]$ at
  roundoff -- the speckle-pair Jacobian column that electric-field-conjugation
  style control rests on.
- The analytic linearization equals `jvp` autodiff **through the vortex**,
  and gradients are finite through every propagator family (the
  half-pixel-offset grids keep the vortex singularity off-sample by
  construction).
- Cross-method agreement (analytic, `jvp`, `jacfwd` at $10^{-12}$), the
  quadratic scaling of the linearization residual, and an
  autodiff-versus-finite-difference check of the detector's reparameterized
  read live in `test_linearize.py`, `test_multichannel.py`, and
  `test_detector.py`.

### Compilation contract

`test_jit_consistency.py` locks in that `jit` and `vmap` change nothing but
speed: the compiled vortex chain and Fresnel hop equal their eager
counterparts at $10^{-15}$, and batched execution (a stack of deformable
mirror commands, the cmft leading batch axis) equals the per-item loop.

### Statistics

`test_stats.py` verifies the modified-Rician intensity statistics against
numerical quadrature (normalization, mean, exponential limit) and against a
Monte-Carlo ensemble of speckle-generator draws; `test_speckle.py` pins the
spectral-synthesis variance identity exactly and the $1/\lambda^2$ chromatic
halo scaling.

## Tier B: the design-survey benchmark

The primary external reference is the HWO Coronagraph Design Survey
(cds_pipeline) **EAC-1 AAVC** design on the EAC-1 segmented aperture,
computed by an independent code (HCIPy). These tests read a locally cached
reference export (`$PHYSICALOPTIX_EAC1_CACHE` or `tests/data/`) and **skip
when it is absent**, so run them before trusting a propagation-core change.

- **Aperture.** The EAC-1 pupil rasterized from the mission YAML matches the
  design-survey FITS pupil to within one part in 256 (the rasterization
  quantum); the only differing pixels sit on segment edges at most one gray
  level apart (`test_eac1_pupil.py`).
- **Telescope PSF.** Scale-fit residual below $10^{-9}$ -- machine precision
  once the overall normalization is fit (`test_eac1_gates.py`).
- **Off-axis PSFs.** Planets at 7 and 15 $\lambda/D$ match to residuals
  below $10^{-4}$ (measured near $10^{-5}$).
- **On-axis null floor.** $3.048\times10^{-11}$ against the reference
  $3.043\times10^{-11}$: 0.2 percent agreement on a $3\times10^{-11}$
  contrast floor. The pointwise null *shape* at this depth is
  implementation-specific (the residual fields differ at the
  $\sim3\times10^{-12}$ contrast level); the aggregate floor that drives
  yield estimates agrees.
- **Broadband.** The band-averaged floor holds the monochromatic null and a
  chromatic off-axis source stays at fixed sky angle while speckles march
  radially (`test_eac1_broadband.py`).
- **Linearization export.** `linearize` reproduces a stored dense-basis
  sensitivity export of the full chain: frequencies to $10^{-12}$, nominal
  field to $10^{-9}$ of peak, sensitivity columns to $10^{-10}$ of peak
  (`test_g_export.py`).
- **Yield input package.** `emit_yip` output is compared field by field
  against a package generated by cds_pipeline itself with matched random
  streams: off-axis and sky products at the $10^{-4}$ level, absolute
  normalization to one part in $10^{3}$, on-axis floor ratio within 1 percent
  (`test_yip_cds_crosscheck.py`). This cross-check has caught real recipe
  bugs (a missing chromatic amplitude factor; a wavelength-sampling range),
  which is the point of keeping it.

## Numerical requirements

The deep-contrast path is **float64-mandatory**: in float32 the dark hole
floors near $3\times10^{-4}$ contrast instead of $10^{-11}$. The test suite
pins itself to CPU float64 for determinism (`tests/conftest.py`); production
use should call `jax.config.update("jax_enable_x64", True)` (or
`hwoutils.enable_x64()`) before building deep-null chains. Sampling regimes
the construction-time gates reject (`on_undersampled="raise"`) are outside
the validated envelope; the gates exist so an undersampled chain fails at
build, not silently at depth.

## What is not yet validated

Honesty about the boundary:

- **No laboratory data.** Nothing here compares against measured testbed
  hardware; all validation is against closed-form physics and an independent
  simulation code. Hardware-anchored validation of segment-level models
  exists in the field (for example PASTIS on HiCAT, Laginja et al. 2022) and
  is the model for what Tier C should eventually look like here.
- **Polarization** is not modeled (a sizing study found the filtered leakage
  floors below the drift floors for the current design regime), and the
  chromatic linearization shortcut is deliberately unimplemented pending its
  own validation.
- Tier B currently covers one design family (the EAC-1 AAVC); other survey
  design families are config-only extensions and inherit only Tier A until
  benchmarked.

## Reproducing

Tier A runs anywhere:

```bash
uv run pytest tests/
```

Tier B activates automatically when the reference cache is present
(`$PHYSICALOPTIX_EAC1_CACHE` pointing at the cds_pipeline EAC-1 export, or
copies under `tests/data/`):

```bash
uv run pytest tests/validation/
```

Each gate asserts the tolerances above, so a regression in the propagation
core surfaces as a failed gate with the measured residual in its message.
